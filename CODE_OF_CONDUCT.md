# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge
We are committed to making participation in this project a harassment-free and helpful experience for everyone. We welcome all people regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Accessibility Commitment
Because one in four people have a disability. Because this is often overlooked, it is important to highlight our commitment to support people with disabilities. 
- **Communication:** We strive to use plain language and provide alternative text for images.
- **Tools:** We prefer using tools and platforms that work well with assistive technology.
- **Support:** If you find a barrier that prevents you from participating, please let us know. We will work with you to find a solution.

## Standards for Behavior
To create a positive environment, please follow these guidelines:

### Be Respectful
Treat others as you would like to be treated. Value the time and effort of other contributors. Be kind even when you disagree.

### Be Professional
Focus on the project goals and the community. Use professional language and avoid personal attacks or inappropriate jokes.

### Be Considerate
Your work will be used by others, and you will depend on the work of others. Any decision you make will affect users and colleagues; take those consequences into account when making decisions.

### Other Positive Behaviors
* Use welcoming and inclusive language.
* Be respectful of different viewpoints and experiences.
* Gracefully accept constructive criticism.
* Focus on what is best for the community.
* Show empathy towards other community members.

## Unacceptable Behavior
The following behaviors are not allowed:
* Sexualized language, imagery, or attention of any kind.
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
* Public or private harassment.
* Publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email address) without their explicit permission.
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Responsibilities of Maintainers
Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take fair and timely action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples include using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an event.

### Consequences
If the Code of Conduct is broken, maintainers may take the following actions:
1.  **Correction:** A private conversation to explain why the behavior was inappropriate.
2.  **Warning:** A formal private warning regarding the behavior and its consequences.
3.  **Temporary Ban:** A temporary removal from the project and its communication channels.
4.  **Permanent Ban:** A permanent removal from the project for repeated or severe violations.
