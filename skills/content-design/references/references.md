## Content Design Guidelines

- **Accessibility for Teams (Gov.uk)** — <https://accessibility-manual.dwp.gov.uk/>  
  Guidance for writing, designing, and building accessible services; includes content design roles and plain-language principles.  
  License: Open Government Licence

- **Intuit Content Design** — <https://contentdesign.intuit.com/>  
  Intuit's design system principles for accessible content strategy, inclusive UX writing, and content guidelines; practical examples from a large-scale product team.

- **BBC Accessibility** — <https://bbc.co.uk/accessibility>  
  The BBC's guidelines and commitment to accessible digital content, covering mobile UX, coding examples, and content design.

## Dyslexia & Font Research

The following three sources are research-based perspectives on the "dyslexia-friendly font" question. They provide evidence and context rather than a single prescriptive answer:

- **PMC — Typography and Dyslexia** (NIH research article) — <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5934461/>  
  Scientific examination of typeface effects on reading performance for readers with dyslexia.  
  License: public-domain

- **The Dyslexia-Friendly Font Myth** (heidigregoryparentadvocacy.com) — <https://www.heidigregoryparentadvocacy.com/post/the-dyslexia-friendly-font-myth>  
  Parent advocate and educator perspective on dyslexia-friendly fonts, examining research evidence and practical considerations.

- **Do Dyslexia Fonts Actually Work?** (Edutopia) — <https://www.edutopia.org/article/do-dyslexia-fonts-actually-work>  
  Balanced educational resource examining research on whether specialized dyslexia fonts improve reading.

## Cognitive Accessibility

- **W3C COGA (Cognitive Accessibility Task Force)** — <https://w3c.github.io/coga/research-modules/Technology-Assisted-Indoor-Navigation-and-Wayfindings.html>  
  W3C research on cognitive needs in spatial orientation and navigation systems; informs plain-language requirements for complex instructions.  
  License: W3C Document License

- **W3C WCAG 2.2 Understanding — Readable (SC 3.1)** — <https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/readable>  
  Overview of all Readable success criteria: language of page (3.1.1), language of parts (3.1.2), unusual words (3.1.3), abbreviations (3.1.4), and reading level (3.1.5).

## Official Standards

- **WCAG 3.1.5 Reading Level (Level AAA)** — <https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/reading-level.html>  
  Supplemental content at lower reading level when text requires advanced ability. Target Grade 8 (US) for broad audiences.

- **WCAG 2.4.6 Headings and Labels (Level AA)** — <https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/headings-and-labels.html>  
  Headings and labels must describe the topic or purpose.

- **WCAG 1.3.3 Sensory Characteristics (Level A)** — <https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/sensory-characteristics.html>  
  Instructions must not rely solely on shape, color, size, visual location, or sound.
